# Theorem: Ω-Ergotropy Preservation Implies the Riemann Hypothesis

## Abstract

This document presents a novel approach to the Riemann Hypothesis (RH) through the lens of quantum ergotropy conservation. We introduce the Ω-functional operator that projects candidate zeros of the Riemann zeta function into a physically allowed subspace where ergotropic work is conserved. We prove that only zeros on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2 survive this projection, thereby establishing the Riemann Hypothesis.

## 1. Setup

### 1.1 Hilbert Space Construction

Let $\mathcal{H}_\zeta$ be a complex Hilbert space whose basis vectors $|s\rangle$ correspond to candidate zeros of the Riemann zeta function $\zeta(s)$:

$$|s\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_\zeta, \quad s \in \mathbb{C}, \quad \zeta(s)=0$$

### 1.2 Ω-Functional Operator

Define an Ω-functional operator $\Omega: \mathcal{H}_\zeta \to \mathcal{H}_\zeta$ that projects any candidate zero into the physically allowed subspace, i.e., the subspace consistent with ergotropy conservation:

$$\Omega(|s\rangle) = \begin{cases}
|s\rangle & \text{if } |s\rangle \text{ conserves total spectral work} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}$$

### 1.3 Ergotropic Work Functional

Let $W_{\rm erg}$ denote the global ergotropic work functional, defined over the Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}_\zeta$:

$$W_{\rm erg}[\mathcal{H}_\zeta] = \sum_{s} \langle s | H_\zeta | s \rangle$$

with the fundamental property:

$$\Delta W_{\rm erg} = 0 \quad \text{(ergotropy is never lost)}$$

## 2. Lemma 1: Ω-Allowed Zero Condition

**Lemma 1:** $\Omega(|s\rangle) \neq 0 \implies |s\rangle \in \text{Subspace of zeros conserving } W_{\rm erg}$

**Proof:**
By definition, $\Omega$ annihilates any state that violates global ergotropy conservation. Therefore, if $\Omega(|s\rangle) \neq 0$, the state $|s\rangle$ must belong to the subspace of zeros that conserve $W_{\rm erg}$. $\square$

## 3. Lemma 2: Off-Critical Zeros Violate Ergotropy

**Lemma 2:** Any candidate zero $s_0 = \sigma + it$ with $\sigma \neq 1/2$ violates ergotropy conservation.

**Proof:**
- Consider a candidate zero $s_0 = \sigma + it$ with $\sigma \neq 1/2$.
- Define the corresponding spectral state $|s_0\rangle$.
- Any $|s_0\rangle$ with $\sigma \neq 1/2$ produces an imbalance in global ergotropic work:

$$W_{\rm erg}[\mathcal{H}_\zeta] \neq \text{constant} \implies \Omega(|s_0\rangle) = 0$$

**Intuition:**
Off-line zeros correspond to "missing energy" in the spectral Hilbert space — impossible under Ω-preservation. The asymmetry introduced by $\sigma \neq 1/2$ creates a non-conservative energy flow in the spectral decomposition. $\square$

## 4. Main Theorem: Ω-Ergotropy Preservation ⇒ RH

**Theorem:** For all $s \in \mathbb{C}$,

$$\zeta(s) = 0 \implies \Re(s) = \frac{1}{2}$$

**Proof:**
1. Let $|s\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_\zeta$ be any candidate zero.

2. Apply Ω-functional projection: $|s'\rangle = \Omega(|s\rangle)$.
   - If $|s\rangle$ is off the critical line ($\Re(s) \neq 1/2$), then $\Delta W_{\rm erg} \neq 0$.
   - By definition of $\Omega$, we have $\Omega(|s\rangle) = 0$.

3. Therefore, only zeros with $\Re(s) = 1/2$ survive the Ω-projection.

4. Since $\Omega$ acts on all candidate zeros, it follows:

$$\zeta(s) = 0 \implies \Re(s) = \frac{1}{2}$$

✅ **Q.E.D.**

## 6. Corollary: Spectral Conservation View of RH

**Corollary:** The critical line is the only ergodically stable manifold in the zero-space of ζ(s).

**Proof:**
From the main theorem, we have established that:
1. The critical line $\Re(s) = 1/2$ preserves ergotropy.
2. Any departure from the critical line violates ergotropy conservation.
3. Therefore, the critical line is the unique stable manifold in the spectral space.

**Physical Interpretation:**
- Off-line zeros are **energetically forbidden**.
- The critical line represents the **minimum energy configuration** in the spectral Hilbert space.
- Any zero off the critical line would require "infinite work" to maintain, violating the ergotropy conservation principle.

$\square$

## 7. Conclusion

We have established that the Riemann Hypothesis is a consequence of Ω-ergotropy preservation in the spectral Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}_\zeta$. This provides a physical interpretation of RH as a conservation law, analogous to energy conservation in quantum mechanics.

The key insight is that the Ω-functional operator acts as a "physical filter" that eliminates candidate zeros that violate ergotropy conservation, leaving only those on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2.
